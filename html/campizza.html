CAMP IZZA REDESIGN

ABOUT CAMP IZZA

Camp Izza is a non-profit summer kids camp located in Irvine. For this project we were helped redesign their website in order to improve the site consistency and information architecture.

THE PROBLEM

The layout of Camp Izza’s website made it hard for users to find information around the site. Along with that, it was also made up of inconsistent components, which is a bad design practice which can confuse users. Users also felt that there was a lack of necessary information on the site, which affected their decision making when deciding whether or not they wanted to register for the camp.

MY TASK

Our team delegated a page for each person to redesign. I was assigned to redesign the Fees page of the website. Everyone contributed with doing research before we started on the redesign.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

For our competitive analysis, I decided to analyze SuperCamp by Quantum Physics, which is an indirect competitor. SuperCamp differs from Camp Izza in that its main focus is on educational programs. Camp Izza focuses on fun and recreation. After analyzing the site, we found that SuperCamp tried to fit too much information on its site, which made it hard to focus on a single thing. This provided us an example of what we didn't want to do for our redesign.

USER SURVEYS & INTERVIEWS

Through our interviews, we were able to learn about potential users’ values. One important thing we discovered was that users were more concerned about who is behind the organization rather than the kind of activities it offered. Throughout the survey, a lot of suggestions the participants made is to add more details to a certain service on Camp Izza’s website and they would not make decisions based on the limited information. This gave us direction on how we wanted to make changes to the website.

PERSONAS

After collecting and analyzing the results from our survey and interviews, we created some personas that would help us with the redesign process later. We created a total of three different personas- a parent, a grandparent, and a guardian.

SKETCHES

To the left is an image of one of the sketches that my team member Sabrina did. Each member on the team did a total of four sketches, each of a different page. Some ideas the sketches gave me was the use of a banner image at the top, and using cards to display the different payment types.

SITE CONSISTENCY

In order to keep our site consistent, one of our members, Xiang Wang, created a style guide that we could follow. The style guide included things like colors, typography, and different site components like the navigation bar, cards, and social media icons.

THE REDESIGN

This is my mockup of the redesigned Fees page. A couple of main changes to take note of would be the text in the banner image informing users about possible payment methods, clearly showing the two different types of financial aid along with consistent button usage, and the use of card components to display the different pricings to keep the site consistent.

LIMITATIONS

As we were only given a couple of weeks for the redesign, we were tight on schedule for multiple iterations of designs. Ideally, it would have been good to be able to convene and discuss certain aspects that we enjoyed about each other’s designs, but only a few of us were able to, so there’s a definite stylistic difference between the pages as we designed each individually and put it all together. Additionally, although all of us had previous experience using Adobe XD for other classes, the skill level varied from member to member which might be apparent in the flows and interactions in our mock-up between the pages.

HEURISTIC EVALUATION

1. Visibility of system status
The system shows its status by indicating which page the user is on by underlining the page name on the navigation bar.

2. Match between system and the real world
All information displayed on the page uses understand wording and uses navigation and buttons that users are already used to.

3. User control and freedom
Our site lacks undo buttons that would've been helpful for the submission form.

4. Consistency and standards
We use standard naming conventions like "Home" or "Tuition", "Lunch Mob" might be confusing for some.

5. Error prevention
Our site did not incorporate any kind of error prevention, which would have been helpful for something like the form submission.

6. Recognition rather than recall
We make it easy for users to sign up for the class of their choice by adding sign up buttons to the bottom of each.

7. Flexibility and efficiency of use
Our site did not provide any kind of shortcuts for advanced users.

8. Aesthetic and minimalist design
To keep a simple design, we only used two different fonts and a couple of colors throughout the site. The use of card components also keeps descriptions short and easy to digest.

9. Help users recognize, diagnose, and recover from errors
We did not include any error recovery because we focused on making page mockups, but not interactions.

10. Help and documentation
Our site includes a Contact page which the users can use if they have any questions. The footer on the Lunch Mob page also has a contact section. Our site lacks documentation.

CONCLUSION

In summary, we updated the website to have a more modern visual design, made site navigation easier, and made information more accessible and detailed. We believe that our redesign substantially improves the current Camp Izza website and would increase client interest and retention in the camp. Due to our constraint on time, there was still many other aspects of usability that we were unable to explore, but overall, we were able to gain a lot of experience relating to UX research.
