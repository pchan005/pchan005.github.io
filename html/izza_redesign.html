About

This was a redesign project in my INF132 course where I, and a four others, were tasked with choosing a website to redesign. The only condition we were given was that the website had to be related to social good. My main role in the project was redesigning the finance page.

Our Goals

1. Improve site consistency and affordances. 2. Change page hierarchy to reflect what is most important.

Step 1: Getting Permission

After choosing the site that my group decided to redesign, we decided to contact the owner/founder of Camp Izza, Dr. Omar Ezzeldine. He agreed to help us and gave us permission to redesign their site logo as well.

Step 2: Collecting Data!

User Surveys

Since this redesign was supposed to enhance user experience, one of our goals was to collect user input and analyze what needed changing. My group created a Google form where we not only gathered data on the user's themself, but also about their experience using Camp Izza's website. There were questions that were multiple choice as well as free response. Once the survey was complete, we sent it out to potential users, like those who were working parents, grandparents, etc.

Creating Personas

The next step we conducted was creating some user personas. For this part, I specifically was in charge of creating three different personas. I thought about the type of people who would want to use the Camp Izza website, and decided to make a brief outline of who those people would be. In the end, I came up with a single mother, a troubled uncle, and a grandparent.

User Interviews

Our second approach to collecting data consisted of conducting two user interviews. But like what was mentioned above, since we were unable to find real users who interacted with Camp Izza, we could only interview potential users. This also restricted the kinds of questions we could ask since they had never actually used the site before.
